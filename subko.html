Subko —
Beyond the Brew

Full creative direction strategy for launching a Mumbai specialty coffee brand into the UK university market. From market research to brand extension executions.

The Brief

An Indian coffee brand wants into the UK

Subko is a specialty coffee roastery and bakehouse founded in Mumbai in 2020. Known for authenticity and rapid growth in India, the brand wanted to penetrate the UK market and raise awareness of Indian coffee — a product that, despite India being the 8th largest coffee producer globally, has almost no global recognition.

The challenge: the UK coffee market is worth £11.3 billion, dominated by Costa, Starbucks, Greggs, and Caffe Nero. Premium pricing, cultural unfamiliarity, and fierce competition all stood in the way.

Research

Understand the market before touching the creative

The research covered PESTEL analysis, full competitor mapping across Costa, Starbucks, Caffe Nero, Pret, and Greggs, a category map plotting accessibility against sustainability, and a semiotic analysis of how existing brands communicate.

Key finding: the market gap is high sustainability combined with high accessibility. Every brand that prioritises sustainability is niche. Every brand that is widely accessible has weak sustainability credentials. Subko's opportunity was to own that intersection.

Creative Concepts

Three routes. One final direction.

Concept 01

"Brewed in Tradition"

Heritage-led. The journey of Indian coffee beans from plantation to cup, told through storytelling and experiential marketing.

Concept 02

"Sustainability on the Go"

Eco-first. Sustainable sourcing, fair trade, eco-conscious packaging made accessible to busy students.

Concept 03

"A Taste of India"

Experiential. Region-specific blends, pop-up events, and tasting sessions bringing India's coffee culture to UK campuses.

The final direction, "Beyond the Brew", synthesised all three. Indian coffee as a cultural experience that is also sustainable and accessible.

Brand Extension

Desi Brew by Subko

Rather than importing Subko wholesale into the UK, I developed a brand extension: Subko's Desi Brew. A sub-brand tailored for the UK university market carrying Indian cultural identity without the disconnection of the parent brand's more Western aesthetic.

The brand identity used a custom logotype blending Latin and Devanagari scripts, an earthy colour palette, Indian folk-art-inspired patterns, and a brand personality described as: disruptive, Indian, innovative. Slogan: "Soil to Soul." Executions included campus poster campaigns, a collapsible stainless steel reusable mug with Indian patterns, seed-embedded takeaway cups, a loyalty stamp card, and a mock Instagram page.
