MA Module University of Leeds 2023 Campaign Strategy & Copywriting

O2 Telefonica
Together, Everywhere

A brand strategy and campaign concept for O2 Telefonica, built to reach 16 to 30 year olds in a market where young people actively ignore telecoms advertising. The solution: weather-reactive outdoor billboards that earn attention by being genuinely useful.

The Brief

Get O2 noticed by people who scroll past everything

The Telecommunications brief set by the University of Leeds MA programme asked students to pick a brand from the sector, identify a real problem it was facing, and develop a creative solution using at least three media channels.

I chose O2 Telefonica. The problem I identified: O2's marketing campaigns were successfully reaching older demographics and driving brand awareness among established customers, but the 16 to 30 year old segment was increasingly brand-unaware. The company's own marketing was actually attracting older audiences and reinforcing that perception gap.

The challenge was to raise brand awareness and value perception among a younger audience without alienating O2's existing base, and without producing another forgettable telecoms ad.

The Insight

Together, Everywhere — but only when it matters

The strategic insight came from looking at what young people actually engage with in public spaces. They tune out static advertising. They engage with things that feel relevant to the exact moment they are in.

Weather is the most universally shared real-time experience. Everyone in a city is experiencing the same weather simultaneously. It creates an instant moment of common ground, which is the perfect vehicle for a brand whose platform is "Together, Everywhere."

The concept: digital outdoor billboards that read live weather data and serve contextual copy tailored to whatever conditions exist at that exact moment. Cold and rainy? The ad acknowledges it and makes O2's connectivity feel like the warm constant. Hot and sunny? Different tone, same message. The billboard talks to you about the moment you are actually in.

Media Strategy

Three channels, one idea

The campaign used three channels working in alignment rather than running parallel executions that happened to share a logo.

Digital OOH (primary): Weather-reactive billboards in high-footfall urban areas and near university campuses. Powered by live weather API data. Copy changes automatically based on temperature, precipitation, and conditions. The billboard is always current, always relevant.

Social media (extension): Time-stamped posts that mirror the billboard copy for the exact weather conditions at the time of posting. Creates a sense of the brand being present and paying attention, in the same moment as the audience.

Visual and hijack campaign (supporting): Secondary executions using the same weather data logic in print and digital. "Hijacking" existing weather-related moments, from weather app interfaces to transport screens, to place O2's message inside a context users are already paying attention to.
