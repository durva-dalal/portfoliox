MA Module University of Leeds 2023 Campaign Strategy

O2 Telefonica
Weather Hijack

Brand strategy for reaching 16 to 30 year olds who ignore telecoms advertising. The solution: weather-reactive digital billboards that earn attention by being genuinely relevant to the moment.

The Brief

Get O2 noticed by people who scroll past everything

The Telecommunications brief from the University of Leeds MA programme asked students to pick a brand from the sector, identify a real problem, and develop a creative solution across at least three channels.

I chose O2 Telefonica. The problem: O2's marketing was successfully reaching older demographics but the 16 to 30 year old segment was increasingly brand-unaware. The existing campaigns were actually reinforcing that perception gap.

The challenge was to raise brand awareness and value perception among a younger audience without producing another forgettable telecoms ad.

The Insight

Together, Everywhere

The strategic insight came from looking at what young people actually engage with in public spaces. They tune out static advertising. They engage with things that feel relevant to the exact moment they are in.

Weather is the most universally shared real-time experience. Everyone in a city is experiencing the same weather simultaneously. It creates an instant moment of common ground, which is the perfect vehicle for a brand whose platform is "Together, Everywhere."

The concept: digital outdoor billboards that read live weather data and serve contextual copy tailored to whatever conditions exist at that exact moment. The billboard always feels current, always relevant.

Media Strategy

Three channels, one idea

Digital OOH (primary): Weather-reactive billboards in high-footfall urban areas and near university campuses. Powered by live weather API data. Copy changes automatically based on temperature, precipitation, and conditions.

Social media (extension): Time-stamped posts that mirror the billboard copy for the exact weather conditions at the time of posting. Creates a sense of the brand being present in the same moment as the audience.

Hijack campaign (supporting): Secondary executions using the same weather data logic in print and digital, placing O2's message inside contexts users are already paying attention to.
